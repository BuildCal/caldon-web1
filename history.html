Family Heritage

"Excellence in construction and craftsmanship is built through dedication, expertise, and an unwavering commitment to quality."

The CALDON™ family heritage represents nearly 70 years of dedicated involvement in the construction and property development industries across New South Wales. Our story is one of continuous learning, adaptation, and unwavering commitment to quality craftsmanship spanning from the late 1980s through today's modern business landscape.

From John Caldon's established leadership with Sorensen & Caldon Builders—contributing to over 1,000 homes built across the Manning Valley and earning five consecutive Master Builders Association Excellence Awards—through to Brad Caldon's innovative approach combining university-level construction management with fine craftsmanship and national media recognition, the family has contributed unique expertise while maintaining core values.

Today's CALDON™ represents the evolution of this accumulated knowledge and experience, bringing together traditional craftsmanship values, formal education, contemporary business acumen, and modern market insight gained through multiple successful ventures and industry recognition.

Our Journey

Family Timeline

1982

Early Foundations

The beginning of what would become decades of family involvement in the NSW construction industry, establishing the foundation of expertise and reputation that continues to guide CALDON™ today.

1988

Sorensen & Caldon Builders

John Caldon's involvement with Sorensen & Caldon Builders becomes established in Taree, beginning over three decades of building excellence in the Manning Valley region and surrounding areas, focusing on quality construction and challenging projects.

1990s

Regional Expansion & Industry Recognition

Sorensen & Caldon Builders expands operations across the Mid North Coast from Taree to Port Macquarie, Coffs Harbour, and Newcastle. The family business earns multiple Housing Industry Association (HIA) and Master Builders Association (MBA) awards, establishing a reputation for taking on challenging builds that other contractors wouldn't attempt.

2001

Industry Crisis Response

Following the collapse of HIH Insurance and the subsequent HOW (Home Owners Warranty) crisis that froze almost $2 billion in NSW construction activity, John Caldon contributes to industry recovery efforts by helping complete unfinished homes on the South Coast of NSW, demonstrating the family's commitment to industry integrity and client protection.

Early 2000s

Strategic Partnerships & Business Growth

Sorensen & Caldon Builders develops strategic partnerships with Dixon Homes and Clarendon Homes, expanding their market reach and construction capabilities. The company builds strong relationships with over 15 specialized suppliers and contractors, creating a reliable network that continues to this day.

2003-2005

Next Generation Education & Career Development

Brad Caldon begins his carpentry apprenticeship through TAFE while working in the family construction business, but chooses to pursue higher education. He completes the Open Foundation program and Bachelor of Construction Management at the University of Newcastle, preparing for an innovative approach to the family business legacy.

2005-2010

Independent Business Development

Brad Caldon establishes his own ventures, choosing to apply his construction expertise and university education in independent enterprises. He begins developing the Caldon Group as a residential building company, demonstrating the family's entrepreneurial spirit and adaptability to modern market conditions.

2010-2015

Business Excellence & Market Leadership

Sorensen & Caldon Builders reaches a milestone of over 1,000 homes built across the Manning Valley and Mid North Coast regions. The company becomes known for completing projects abandoned by other builders, earning trust from insurance companies and the NSW Government for their reliability and expertise.

2015

CALDON™ Established

Brad Caldon establishes CALDON™ as a dedicated property development and investment company, bringing together decades of family expertise with modern business practices and strategic market positioning across NSW.

2016-2018

Innovation in Craftsmanship

Brad and his partner Tori found HowsThatHouse in a two-car garage at 13 Cornwall Street, Taree, specializing in handcrafted timber furniture and detailed joinery using coastal hardwoods. The business rapidly evolves from garage startup to professional workshop with Italian precision machinery, showcasing the evolution of traditional craftsmanship into contemporary markets.

2019

National Recognition & Industry Awards

HowsThatHouse achieves breakthrough success through The Block TV partnership with contestants Deb and Andy Saunders, leading to praise from TV personalities Keith and Dan. Simultaneously, Sorensen & Caldon Builders begins its streak of five consecutive Master Builders Association Excellence in Building Awards (2019-2023), cementing both branches of the family legacy in their respective fields.

2020

Business Transition & Pandemic Adaptation

John Caldon transitions from direct ownership of Sorensen & Caldon Builders, marking the end of family business ownership while maintaining the Caldon legacy of excellence. Meanwhile, HowsThatHouse adapts to pandemic challenges by growing their social media presence, adding over 2,000 Instagram followers and developing national online sales channels.

2021-2022

Community Leadership & Digital Growth

John Caldon becomes president of Team Taree (formerly Taree Business Chamber), transitioning his decades of business expertise toward community leadership, economic development, and disaster relief coordination. HowsThatHouse secures distribution deal with The Block Shop, now generating 30% of revenue from national sales while maintaining local craftsmanship excellence.

Present

Multi-Generational Excellence

CALDON™ and HowsThatHouse represent the continuation of nearly 70 years of accumulated knowledge, relationships, and expertise. The family tradition of excellence continues across multiple industries: construction consultancy, property development, fine furniture craftsmanship, and community leadership. From over 1,000 homes built to national TV recognition, the Caldon legacy demonstrates how traditional values can evolve to meet contemporary opportunities.

Our Evolution

The CALDON™ Journey

"True excellence emerges when traditional expertise meets modern innovation, creating new possibilities while honoring proven values."

The establishment of CALDON™ in 2015 represented the natural evolution of nearly 70 years of family involvement in NSW construction and property development. Rather than simply continuing traditional paths, CALDON™ was founded upon a solid foundation of accumulated expertise, established relationships, and proven capabilities enhanced by formal education, industry partnerships, and contemporary business strategies.

This heritage provides CALDON™ with distinct advantages: deep understanding of quality construction practices from over 1,000 residential projects, intimate knowledge of regional markets through strategic partnerships, the wisdom gained from multiple market cycles including industry crises, and the innovation that comes from combining traditional expertise with university education and national media recognition.

Our approach honors traditional values—quality, integrity, and genuine service—while embracing contemporary business practices and modern market opportunities. This blend of heritage and innovation, coupled with formal education, independent entrepreneurial experience, and national recognition, positions CALDON™ to deliver exceptional results in today's competitive property development landscape while maintaining the family legacy of excellence.
